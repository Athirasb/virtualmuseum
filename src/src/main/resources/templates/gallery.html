What is ART!


Art is a multifaceted and profound concept that embodies the creative expression of human imagination, skill, and emotion. It encompasses a wide range of activities and outputs, from painting, sculpture, and literature to music, dance, and digital media. Art serves as a powerful medium for conveying complex ideas, emotions, and narratives, often transcending the limitations of language. It can be a reflection of the artist's inner world, a commentary on society, or an exploration of beauty and aesthetics. Throughout history, art has played a crucial role in cultural and societal development, preserving traditions, challenging norms, and fostering innovation. It not only provides aesthetic pleasure and inspiration but also encourages critical thinking and empathy by allowing us to see the world from different perspectives. In essence, art is a vital aspect of human existence, enriching our lives and connecting us through shared experiences and universal themes.

famous artists

Many famous artists throughout history, each renowned for their unique contributions to the world of art.

(1452–1519)

Leonardo da Vinci

he is best known for his dramatic and expressive artwork, Leonardo also conducted dozens of carefully thought out experiments and created futuristic inventions that were groundbreaking for the time.

(1475–1564)

Michelangelo

Michelangelo Buonarroti as one of the greatest artists of all time. During his prodigious career he sculpted the Pieta in Rome and the David in Florence even designed the dome for St. Peter's Basilica, to name just a few.

(1483–1520)

Raphael

Raphael is probably most famous for his paintings, including Madonna in the Meadow (1505/06), School of Athens (c. 1508–11), Sistine Madonna (1512/13), The Transfiguration (1516–20).

(1853–1890)

Vincent van Gogh

He is now famed for the great vitality of his works which are characterised by expressive and emotive use of brilliant colour and energetic application of impastoed paint.Known for: "Starry Night," "Sunflowers," "Irises"

(1970–)

Julie Mehretu

Mehretu's paintings are constructed through a labor-intensive additive process in which successive layers of lines and thick streams of paint are separated by coats of transparent acrylic.

(1969–)

Kara Walker

Kara Elizabeth Walker is an American contemporary painter, silhouettist, printmaker, installation artist, filmmaker, and professor who explores race, gender, sexuality, violence, and identity in her work.

(1943–)

R. Crumb

Crumb is an American counterculture comic book artist and social satirist, known for his distinctive artwork and excellent marriage of drawing and narrative and for creating such well-known characters as Fritz the Cat and Mr. Natural.

(1969–)

Cecily Brown

Brown now lives and works in New York City, and has had dozens of exhibitions,the Metropolitan Museum of Art held Cecily Brown: Death and the Maid, which was “the first full-breadth museum survey in New York” of Brown's career.

(1968–)

David Shrigley

Shrigley first came to prominence in the 1990s for his distinct line drawings, surreal and darkly humorous subject matter almost childlike style.David Shrigley's most popular works have performed well , with some going for well over their original prices.
